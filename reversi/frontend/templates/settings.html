AI Ladder

Difficulty Overview

Easy - Random

Chooses a legal move without deeper planning, making it the most forgiving opponent.

Medium - Greedy

Looks for immediately strong-looking moves, especially corners, but does not search ahead.

Advanced - Hybrid

Uses a richer heuristic that blends board weights, mobility, corners, and risky-square avoidance.

Hard - Minimax

Searches future positions with the engine evaluator, so it is slower but usually the toughest matchup.

How to Play

Rules at a Glance

Objective

Finish the game with more discs of your color on the board than your opponent when no more legal moves remain for either side.

What Makes a Move Legal

A move is legal only if your new disc flips at least one opponent disc. To do that, your placed disc must bracket one or more opposing discs in a straight line with another disc of your own color.

Straight Lines Only

Brackets can happen horizontally, vertically, or diagonally. If the line is broken, or if no opponent discs are trapped between your discs, the move is illegal.

How Flipping Works

Every opponent disc trapped between the disc you place and another one of your discs flips immediately. A single move can flip discs in several directions at once.

If No Discs Flip

If placing a disc would not flip anything, that square is not allowed. Legal-move hints on the Play page mark only the squares that satisfy this rule.

Pass Rule

If it is your turn and you have no legal move, you must pass. The game handles that automatically and returns play to your opponent.

Game End and Winner

The game ends when neither player can move. The winner is the player with more discs on the board, and equal totals produce a draw.

Undo and Replay

Local PvP can enable undo and replay. Replay mode steps through stored history without changing the actual finished game record.

Simulation Lab

The Lab runs repeated AI-vs-AI benchmarks, alternates colors for fairness, and summarizes the matchup with scores, rates, and charts.

Beginner Strategy

First Things to Look For

Corners Matter

Corner discs can never be flipped, so taking a corner is usually one of the strongest moves available.

Risky Squares

Squares next to corners, especially x-squares and c-squares, can be dangerous because they often give the opponent an easy corner afterward.

More Flips Is Not Always Better

A move that flips many discs right away may open strong replies. Sometimes the better move flips fewer discs but leaves a safer position.

Limit Opponent Mobility

Strong play often comes from reducing how many legal moves your opponent has next, even if your immediate score gain looks smaller.
