Why It Stands Out

A game project with real analytical depth

Strategy comparison, not only strategy play

The Lab makes this more than a playable board. Matchups can be benchmarked, compared across remembered runs, and reviewed through dominance summaries and measured pairwise relationships.

Move-by-move analysis already built in

The engine tracks progression metrics and post-game summaries, which supports charted control shifts, flip patterns, and representative-game inspection instead of only showing a final score.

Product thinking behind the interface

The app is structured as a real multi-page product shell with distinct surfaces for play, experimentation, and guidance, rather than a single overloaded screen.

Built to be explainable and extensible

Simulation, analytics, and UI concerns are separated cleanly enough that the project can evolve into richer dashboards and stronger analysis features without rewriting the core experience.

About Me

Why I built this project

Industrial Engineering, analytics, and product perspective

I come from an Industrial Engineering and Management background, with a strong interest in algorithms, AI, analytics, optimization, and systems thinking. I am especially drawn to products that turn complex logic into something clear, usable, and decision-oriented.

This project reflects that mindset: it is not only about implementing Reversi correctly, but about designing the surrounding product surface so strategy behavior can be explored, compared, and understood.

A portfolio piece that combines play, experimentation, and software design

Reversi gave me a compact but meaningful space to bring several interests together: game logic, AI strategy design, benchmarking, analytics, and interface polish. The goal was to build something technically strong enough to stand up as an engineering project, while still feeling approachable and intentional as a user-facing product.

That combination matters to me. I enjoy building systems that are rigorous under the hood, but also shaped by product thinking, clarity, and a good user experience.
